# PRODUCT.md

## Register

**brand**

This is a personal portfolio. The design *is* the artifact — typography, pacing, and feel carry as much weight as the words. Treat every surface as a brand surface: nothing here is a utility tool optimizing for task throughput.

## Owner

Nadia (nadia@marinachain.io). The site speaks in first person; "I" not "we." No fake company plural.

## Product purpose

A small, durable home on the internet for Nadia's work and thinking. One canonical link to send to anyone — peers, would-be collaborators, people who Googled the name after a conversation. Built to age well; built so a reader leaves with a clear sense of who Nadia is and what she pays attention to.

It is not a job-board profile, not a feed, not a launch page.

## Audience

Primary: people across the tech industry — engineers, designers, founders, operators — who land here from a referral, a conversation, or a search. They are intelligent, scan fast on the first pass, and read deeply only if the first pass earned it.

They are *not* recruiters working a pipeline, and they are *not* crypto degens looking for an airdrop. Designing for either of those audiences would push the site toward a template Nadia is explicitly trying to avoid.

What they want: signal in under ten seconds (who, what kind of work), then optional depth (essays, project detail) if the signal landed.

## Content

The site holds four things, in roughly this order of weight:

1. **About / bio.** Who Nadia is, in her own voice. Not a resume.
2. **Selected work.** Curated project entries with context and outcomes. Quality over completeness — a few projects told well, never an exhaustive list.
3. **Writing / essays.** Long-form posts and notes. The piece of the site that compounds over time.
4. **Contact / links.** Email and the handful of off-site places worth pointing at.

No blog feed firehose, no public RSS-of-everything, no "now" page unless one is deliberately added later.

## Voice

**Plain and warm.** Conversational without being chatty. Unguarded without oversharing. Writes like a thoughtful person talking to one other person, not like a brand addressing a market.

Concretely:
- First person, contractions allowed, sentences vary in length.
- No corporate hedging ("leverage," "solutions," "empower").
- No false modesty and no chest-thumping. State what's true.
- Headings are descriptive, not clever. The cleverness is in the prose, not the labels.
- Em dashes are banned across the project — use commas, colons, semicolons, or periods.

## Strategic principles

These are the load-bearing decisions. When a design choice is ambiguous, return to these.

1. **Typography carries the brand.** Color is restrained; layout is calm. The signature is in type — pairing, scale, measure, leading, ligatures. If a page is removed of color entirely and still reads as distinctly Nadia's, the design is working.
2. **Slow reading is the goal.** Generous measure (65–75ch), generous vertical rhythm, generous margins. The site should reward someone who stops and reads, not punish them for not skimming.
3. **One canonical voice across surfaces.** Bio, project intro, essay, 404 page — same person writing. No marketing voice that appears only on the home page.
4. **Built to age, not to chase.** No trend-of-the-month effects. No dependence on a single hero animation. Choices should look right in three years.
5. **Restraint is a flex.** White space, single accent, no decorative chrome. Confidence shown by what's removed.

## Anti-references

The site must not be mistaken for any of the following. If a draft starts drifting toward one, rework it.

- **SaaS landing template.** Gradient hero, three feature cards, testimonial carousel, stacked CTAs. None of this. No "trusted by" logo wall.
- **Crypto-bro neon.** Neon-on-black, animated gradients, glow effects, $TOKEN energy, "WAGMI" voice. Marinachain is in Nadia's bio, not the visual identity.
- **Standard dev portfolio.** Dark mode + monospace body + GitHub-green accent + terminal cursor flourish. The default every engineer ships. Specifically avoided.
- **Corporate / agency site.** Stock photography, navy blue, sans-serif everywhere, "We help businesses scale" copy. No.

## What "done" looks like

Open the home page on a desktop, on a phone, in a dim room and a bright one. In every case, the first impression should read as: *a thoughtful person, who pays attention to craft, made this themselves.* Not: *a designer template was applied to a person.*

## /play is an exempt sandbox

The `/play` route is the one place where the rules above do **not** apply. It is an experimental space for one-off pieces that explore a different aesthetic, theme, or technique on purpose. Currently `/play` houses an ASCII jellyfish on a dark-sea background, in monospace, with no fanta pink. That breaks several brand laws on this page; that's the point.

Future `/play` pieces should be similarly committed to their own register. Don't apply main-brand critiques to `/play` and don't bleed `/play` aesthetics into the main brand. The home page stays editorial-typographic; the sandbox stays the sandbox.
